On reproducibility

Every analysis is written as version-controlled R with a documented pipeline. That means the result is traceable to the code, the code is re-runnable, and the work stands up to an audit or a reviewer's scrutiny — the same discipline expected of a peer-reviewed publication or a regulatory submission.

I've spent my research career turning large, complex health data into clear answers. My daily work links Danish nationwide registries and long-follow-up biomarker cohorts through Statistics Denmark, using survival analysis, Poisson regression, causal inference and multiple imputation — all in reproducible R.

That work is peer-reviewed: my research on biomarker-based cohorts and long-term health outcomes has been published in journals including Environment International. The same rigour a journal demands is what I bring to client work.

I take on a limited number of freelance projects alongside my research. If your team needs extra analysis capacity — without the overhead of a full-service firm or a full-time hire — that's exactly the gap I fill.
